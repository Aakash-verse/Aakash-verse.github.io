About
01

A new home for ML theory

AMICO is an international academic conference dedicated to the theoretical foundations of machine learning. Inaugurated in 2026, the conference brings together researchers working on the mathematical and algorithmic underpinnings of modern AI.

From convex and non-convex optimization to statistical learning, generalization bounds, and the theory of deep learning — AMICO spans the full breadth of rigorous ML research.

All accepted papers will be published in open-access proceedings. The conference will feature invited talks, contributed papers, and poster sessions, all hosted in an accessible online format.

Topics
02

Scope & research areas

⚙️

Optimization Theory

Convex and non-convex optimization, convergence rates, first- and second-order methods, saddle-point problems.

📐

Statistical Learning Theory

PAC learning, VC dimension, Rademacher complexity, generalization bounds, online learning.

🧠

Deep Learning Theory

Implicit regularization, loss landscape geometry, overparameterization, neural tangent kernels.

📊

High-Dimensional Statistics

Concentration inequalities, sparse estimation, random matrix theory, compressive sensing.

🎲

Stochastic Optimization

SGD and variants, variance reduction, noise models, asynchronous and distributed methods.

🔒

Robust & Safe Learning

Adversarial robustness, distribution shift, differential privacy, algorithmic fairness.

🔁

Reinforcement Learning Theory

Regret bounds, sample complexity, exploration-exploitation, policy gradient methods.

🌐

Kernels & Gaussian Processes

Reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces, spectral methods, Bayesian nonparametrics, kernel approximations.

🧩

Computational Complexity

Hardness of learning, query complexity, communication complexity in learning settings.

05

Share your research with the world

AMICO welcomes original contributions in all areas of machine learning theory. We seek work that advances our mathematical understanding of learning algorithms, and encourage submissions from both established researchers and early-career scientists.

Submissions should be in anonymous format following the provided LaTeX template. The review process is double-blind and single-track.

Fully Online Conference

AMICO 2026 will be held entirely online, making world-class machine learning theory accessible to researchers everywhere regardless of geography or travel budget.

We will host live streamed invited talks with Q&A, virtual poster sessions, and dedicated networking channels. Platform details will be announced closer to the conference.
