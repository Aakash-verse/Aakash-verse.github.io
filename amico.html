About the Conference

A New Venue for
Theoretical ML

AMICO (the Aakash and Maria International Conference on Optimization) is a new annual conference dedicated to theoretical research across all areas of machine learning. Our first edition will be held online in December 2026.

The conference aims to foster rigorous, mathematically-grounded work on the foundations of modern machine learning — from the geometry of loss landscapes and the dynamics of gradient-based algorithms, to statistical learning theory, generalization bounds, and beyond.

AMICO welcomes original contributions at the intersection of optimization, statistics, high-dimensional probability, and theoretical computer science as they relate to machine learning. We are committed to accessibility, open science, and a welcoming community for researchers at all career stages.

Topics of Interest

∇

Optimization for Machine Learning

Convergence theory, adaptive methods, variance reduction, second-order methods, non-convex landscapes, and implicit regularization.

𝒱

Statistical Learning Theory

Generalization bounds, VC theory, PAC learning, Rademacher complexity, uniform convergence, and information-theoretic limits.

λ

Deep Learning Theory

Neural tangent kernels, loss landscape geometry, overparameterization, implicit bias of gradient descent, and expressivity.

σ

High-Dimensional Statistics

Random matrix theory, compressed sensing, sparse estimation, matrix factorization, and concentration of measure.

π

Probabilistic Methods & Sampling

MCMC, Langevin dynamics, variational inference, diffusion models from a theoretical perspective, and Bayesian learning.

ε

Online Learning & Bandits

Regret minimization, multi-armed bandits, adversarial learning, game theory, and online convex optimization.

𝒢

Federated & Distributed Learning

Communication complexity, heterogeneity, differential privacy, decentralized optimization, and Byzantine robustness.

ρ

Reinforcement Learning Theory

Sample complexity, exploration-exploitation, policy gradient methods, value function approximation, and MDPs.

η

Computational Complexity

Hardness of learning problems, algorithmic lower bounds, cryptographic connections, and query complexity.

Call for Papers

Paper Submission

AMICO 2026 invites submissions of original research on the theory of machine learning. Papers should present novel theoretical results, including proofs of convergence, generalization bounds, lower bounds, or algorithmic guarantees. Both long and short papers are welcome.

All submissions will undergo double-blind peer review. Authors should ensure their papers are anonymized before submission. Previously published or concurrently submitted work is not eligible for submission.

Submissions must be formatted using the official AMICO LaTeX style file, which will be made available on the submission platform. Papers exceeding the page limit will not be reviewed.

The submission portal will open shortly. Please check back here or sign up for email updates.
